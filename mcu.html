CROSSFIT IN THE MIDDLE EAST

Today’s tid bit is brought to you by Athlete Daily.

THE STORY

Shaikha Al Qassimi is a Red Bull athlete, Lululemon ambassador and competitive CrossFit athlete. And the 27-year-old citizen of United Arab Emirates is smashing age-old gender notions as part of a still-emerging cultural shift.

HOW’D SHE GET STARTED?

Drawing inspiration from her social media following, Al Qassimi — who started CrossFit after watching an online video in 2013 — is the owner of Platform gym in Dubai. A CrossFit Level 1 trainer, Al Qassimi is on a mission to change the UAE fitness movement and forge a path for future generations.

“Socially, it was very hard when I first started – it still is at some points – having to explain why I spend so much time in the gym or why I train so hard or so much,” Al Qassimi recently told The National. “This is a passion, a dream. People will always question what they don’t understand.”

HOW POPULAR IS CROSSFIT IN THE MIDDLE EAST?

Very. The past three years has seen incredible growth in the region in CrossFit. Just a simple Google search for affiliates in the UAE alone turns out more than 15 different options.

One of the big reasons for the growth is the annual Dubai Fitness Championship, which has seen the likes of Games athletes Noah Ohlsen and Annie Thorisdottir take home the prize. And it’s sizable purse money as both of last year’s champs won more than $54,000 in the December competition.

The prince of Dubai, His Highness Sheikh ‘Majid bin Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum puts on the competition and is reportedly very into functional fitness.

The event does more than bring CrossFit royalty abroad. It also helps foster real change for women in fitness. Al Qassimi is among those gearing up to compete in this competition next month.

SO, IS CROSSFIT HERE THE SAME AS CROSSFIT THERE?

Pretty much. Though there are much more women’s-only fitness classes, which are often created out of demand. It’s not that men and women working out together is against the law, but it is more of a cultural issue.

When a Games athlete fails a rep it doesn’t look any different than one of us, just watch Tia-Clair Toomey. Chris Spealler isn’t ruling out another run at the CrossFit Games if he were to qualify for the 35-39 Masters bracket. Reebok designed the new Legacy Lifters with the help of world record lifter CJ Cummings. Kalynn Pepito back squats 295 pounds 9 days before the American Open. Jezza Uepa back squatted 1,036 pounds. This 14-year-old Aussie girl became the youngest person to ski to the North Pole and she’s doing CrossFit to train for her next big adventure. Rogue Fitness will be the official manufacturer for USA Weightlifting for the next four years. Jared Enderton snatched 326 pounds (video coming soon).

CHALK UP AFFILIATES

Athletes at CrossFit Harwood Heights in Chicago hosted the “CrossFit Harwood Heights Does Zumba To Feed The Homeless” fundraiser over the weekend, raising $1,000 to feed local area homeless.

Not quite an affiliate but the social studies teacher at Discovering Choices, a Calgary Board of Education alternative high school for at-risk kids, has weights and rings throughout his classroom so his students can stay active. They can do pushups, ring-dip challenges. They do all sorts of weird stuff and challenge each other to do stuff. And I say, ‘OK, you’ve distracted me long enough. You can get back to doing your schoolwork.’ I’m easily distracted – I’m just like they are.” Oh and he coaches at CrossFit Calgary.
